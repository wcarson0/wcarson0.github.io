Senior Design Project

In this project, my group and I designed a Bipropellant Liquid Rocket Engine Test Stand for Triton Space Technologies.

Project Overview

During the 2022-2023 academic year at Boston University, I had the privilege of being part of Team 15's senior capstone project, collaborating closely with Triton Space Technologies. Our collective effort aimed to design and develop a test stand for their bipropellant liquid rocket engine, facilitating thorough testing and analysis. The project demanded a solution that enabled remote power and monitoring while ensuring the safe and efficient delivery of fuel to the engine.

Triton Space Technologies, a distinguished design and manufacturing company based in Massachusetts, entrusted us with the task of creating a custom test stand to support their bipropellant rocket engine testing. With budget limitations preventing physical manufacturing, our team shifted its focus towards delivering a comprehensive design report, encompassing detailed manufacturing drawings and CAD models.

The test stand we designed comprised five major components: a frame, propellant tanks, pressurant system, feed system, and data acquisition system. The frame, meticulously constructed from two welded modules secured with bolts, provided a sturdy foundation. Custom-manufactured spherical tanks were engineered to hold the essential ethanol and nitrous oxide fuels required for engine operation. To drive the fuel through the system, K-sized nitrogen pressurant tanks were integrated, utilizing strain relief brackets to ensure stability. A carefully designed feed system, incorporating a range of valves, allowed for precise control of the flow while also providing emergency dumping capabilities if necessary. Monitoring the flow, temperatures, and pressures were an array of strategically placed sensors, seamlessly integrated into the system. All data was efficiently captured and managed by the data acquisition (DAQ) system, which offered power and control capabilities for specific valves. To safeguard the DAQ system from potential propellant tank explosions, blast plates were implemented, ensuring operational resilience.

As a team, we wholeheartedly contributed to the design and development of the test stand, resulting in a robust and efficient system that met Triton Space Technologies' requirements. Through the comprehensive design report, we provided the client with the necessary manufacturing drawings and CAD models for future implementation. Our collective expertise and dedication demonstrated our capabilities in engineering design and problem-solving, fostering a successful collaboration with Triton Space Technologies.
