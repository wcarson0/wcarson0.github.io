About Me As a recent Boston University graduate with a Bachelor of Science degree in Mechanical Engineering, I have cultivated a foundation in problem-solving and technical prowess. Throughout my academic journey, I have diligently sharpened my proficiency in a diverse array of programming languages, such as MATLAB, C++, C, Linux, Python, and Java. This comprehensive programming repertoire empowers me with the confidence and dexterity needed to deftly navigate intricate engineering challenges. Moreover, I have garnered invaluable hands-on experience with the Computer-Aided Design software, SOLIDWORKS, where I acquired adeptness in conducting Finite Element Analysis (FEA), a powerful technique for evaluating structural integrity.

During my time at Boston University, I also had the privilege of being a part of the Men's Lacrosse team, where I played an integral role as a Division-I athlete. Through my lacrosse journey, I cultivated invaluable life skills that continue to shape my approach to personal and professional endeavors. These skills include:

Time Management:
As the only engineering student on the BU lacrosse team among 65 players, I faced the unique challenge of balancing the demands of a rigorous academic curriculum in Mechanical Engineering with a 20+ hour per week commitment to training, traveling and competing in lacrosse. This experience ingrained in me the utmost importance of effective time management. By meticulously allocating my time and prioritizing tasks, I not only excelled academically but also dedicated myself fully to the sport, achieving high levels of productivity in both domains. Successfully navigating this intricate schedule exemplifies my exceptional time management skills and ability to thrive in demanding multifaceted environments.

Managing stress in high-pressure situations:
As a lacrosse player, I experienced numerous high-pressure situations on the field. Whether it was a crucial game or a challenging academic deadline, I developed the ability to perform under pressure and manage stress effectively. This skill has proven invaluable in my engineering career, where I face demanding projects and deadlines with composure and resilience.

Communication skills:
Being part of a lacrosse team required constant communication with teammates, coaches, and support staff. I learned to process and incorporate feedback, synthesize information, and convey clear and concise messages. This skill set has strengthened my ability to collaborate effectively in team environments and enhanced my professional interactions in engineering settings.

Senior Design Project - Bipropellant Liquid Rocket Engine Test Stand for Triton Space Technologies

During my senior design project at Boston University, I collaborated with Triton Space Technologies to create a custom test stand for their bipropellant rocket engine. Our deliverables included a comprehensive design report with manufacturing drawings, CAD models, and detailed documentation for future development.

Cartesian Motion System

Designed and constructed a claw machine, utilizing a compact cartesian motion system with 2.5 degrees of freedom. This project incorporated linear drives, stepper motors, servo motors, and pulleys into the design, successfully fitting the entire project into a backpack.

Moped Repair

As a side project, I successfully restored a non-functional moped by addressing the ignition issue through precise soldering of internal wires and replacing worn brake pads. Additionally, I performed an impressive repair that showcased my mechanical engineering skills.
