Cartesian Motion System

Designed and built a compact cartesian motion system in the form of a claw machine; demonstrating knowledge of linear drives, stepper motors, servo motors, and pulleys.

Project Overview

During my project design class at Boston University, my fellow group members and I embarked on an exciting endeavor to create a compact cartesian motion system in the form of a claw machine. Guided by our knowledge of linear drives, stepper motors, servo motors, and pulleys, we meticulously designed and built a functional and visually appealing claw machine. Through thorough planning, collaboration, and the application of CAD modeling and design optimization, we successfully constructed a claw machine that surpassed our expectations in terms of functionality and aesthetics. This project demonstrated our proficiency in integrating various mechanical elements and translating theoretical concepts into a practical and engaging project.

In the final presentation, my fellow group members and I conducted a live demonstration of our completed model, showcasing the smooth operation and impressive capabilities of our claw machine. This project not only strengthened my understanding of cartesian motion systems but also honed my teamwork and problem-solving skills, preparing me for future challenges in the field of mechanical engineering. By collaborating and leveraging our individual strengths, we successfully delivered a remarkable claw machine that exemplified our collective dedication, knowledge, and passion for engineering.
